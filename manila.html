What to Do in Manila

Manila, the capital city of the Philippines, offers a wide range of activities and attractions for visitors to enjoy. Here are some popular things to do in Manila:

1. Explore Intramuros

Intramuros is the historic district of Manila, known for its well-preserved Spanish colonial architecture and fortifications. You can walk along the walls of Fort Santiago, visit the beautiful San Agustin Church, which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and explore the various museums, gardens, and plazas within Intramuros. It's a great place to learn about the rich history and culture of Manila.

2. Visit Rizal Park

Rizal Park, also known as Luneta Park, is a historical park dedicated to the country's national hero, Jose Rizal. It's a sprawling park with gardens, monuments, and memorials, including the Rizal Monument, which marks the spot where Rizal was executed. Rizal Park is a significant landmark in Manila and a place for reflection and appreciation of Philippine history.

3. Take a trip to National Museum Complex

The National Museum Complex in Manila consists of three museums: the National Museum of Fine Arts, the National Museum of Anthropology, and the National Museum of Natural History. These museums house a vast collection of artworks, artifacts, and specimens that showcase the rich cultural and natural heritage of the Philippines. It's a great place to learn about the country's art, history, and biodiversity.

4. Shop and dine in Bonifacio High Street

Bonifacio High Street is a vibrant shopping and dining district in Manila. It's known for its modern architecture, trendy boutiques, restaurants, and cafes. It's a great place to shop for local and international brands, indulge in delicious Filipino cuisine, and enjoy the lively atmosphere of Manila's urban scene.

5. Visit Manila Ocean Park

Manila Ocean Park is a popular marine-themed park that offers a variety of attractions and activities for the whole family. You can explore the underwater world through aquarium exhibits, enjoy exciting animal shows, interact with marine animals, and even try some thrilling rides like the Oceanarium's glass bottom boat ride. It's a fun and educational experience for visitors of all ages.

6. Discover Fort Santiago

Fort Santiago is a 16th-century citadel located within Intramuros. It's a significant historical landmark that served as a defense fortress during the Spanish colonial period. Today, it's a well-preserved site where you can explore the fort's dungeons, ramparts, and gardens, and learn about its rich history through exhibits and displays. It's a must-visit destination for history buffs and those interested in Manila's colonial past.

7. Visit Binondo, Manila's Chinatown

Binondo, located near Intramuros, is the oldest Chinatown in the world. It's a vibrant district known for its bustling streets, Chinese temples, and authentic Chinese cuisine. You can explore the narrow alleys, visit historic temples like the Binondo Church, and indulge in delicious Chinese food from various street vendors and restaurants. It's a great place to experience Manila's multicultural heritage and taste some unique flavors.
